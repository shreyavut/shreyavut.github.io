Hello! I'm Shreya (pronounced sh-ray-uh).

I'm a young, passionate journalist exploring my interests in data and the social sciences.

I'm studying journalism and political science at the University of Maryland, with a minor in geospatial information science. I'm hoping to prepare myself for a career that integrates feature writing, data analysis and mapping skills in investigative journalism.

I'm currently working with the data and investigations team at The Dallas Morning News.

I also edit data and and news for The Diamondback, UMD's independent student newspaper. Before that, I wrote and managed enterprise projects, including a series I spearheaded on campus accessibility, which was featured in a Washington Post column. I've also written about city and campus development, housing policy, zoning laws, elections and county news.

This past spring, I collaborated on the data team for a project about state lotteries at the Howard Center for Investigative Journalism. I've also dug into historical archives and reported on the role that white newspapers played in aiding and abetting lynchings for the Howard Center.

I'm comfortable working in R, ESRI platforms, HTML and CSS. I'm learning to use JavaScript, QGIS and Adobe Illustrator and am always looking for ways to make better maps and write more efficient code.

When I'm not working, you can find me curating Spotify playlists or watching cooking competition shows.
